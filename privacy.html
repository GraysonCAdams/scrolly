Privacy Policy

Effective date: February 28, 2026

Open-Source Software Notice: Scrolly is open-source software distributed under the MIT License. This privacy policy applies to the official instance operated by the Scrolly project maintainers. Self-hosted instances are independently operated, may modify the source code, and are governed by their own privacy policies. The Scrolly maintainers have no control over and accept no responsibility for the privacy practices of third-party deployments.

1. What We Collect

Account Information

  • Phone number — required for account verification and SMS-based clip sharing. Stored in hashed or plaintext form in the application database.
  • Username — a display name you choose during onboarding.
  • Profile picture — optional, processed client-side and stored on the server.

Content You Share

  • Video and music clips — links you submit (via the app or SMS) and the downloaded media files.
  • Comments, reactions, and captions — text and emoji you attach to clips.

Automatically Collected

  • Session tokens — HTTP-only cookies used for authentication.
  • Push notification subscriptions — Web Push endpoint and encryption keys, if you opt in.
  • Basic server logs — IP addresses and request metadata retained temporarily for security and debugging.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • Phone verification & login — we send one-time SMS codes to verify your identity.
  • Clip sharing via SMS — when you text a link to our Twilio number, we process it and reply with a confirmation.
  • Push notifications — to notify you of new clips, comments, and reactions (configurable in settings).
  • Operating the service — displaying content in your group feed, managing your account, and maintaining service reliability.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or sale to third parties.

3. Third-Party Services

  • Twilio — processes SMS messages (verification codes and inbound clip sharing). Twilio receives your phone number and message content. See Twilio's Privacy Policy.
  • Web Push (browser vendor) — push notification delivery is handled by your browser vendor (e.g., Google, Mozilla, Apple).

No analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs are used.

4. Data Storage & Retention

  • Data is stored in a SQLite database on the server hosting the instance.
  • Downloaded media files are stored on the server filesystem.
  • Verification codes expire after 10 minutes and are not reusable.
  • We retain your account data for as long as your account exists. If your group admin removes you or you request deletion, your data is removed.

5. Data Sharing

Your content (clips, comments, reactions) is visible to members of your Scrolly group. We do not share personal information with third parties except:

  • As required to operate the service (Twilio for SMS).
  • As required by law or valid legal process.

6. Your Rights

  • You may update your username, phone number, or profile picture in the app settings.
  • You may disable push notifications at any time.
  • You may request deletion of your account and associated data by contacting the group administrator or the project maintainers.

7. Security

We use HTTP-only session cookies, timing-safe OTP comparison, rate-limited verification, and Twilio request signature validation. However, no system is perfectly secure. The software is provided "as is" under the MIT License.

8. Children

Scrolly is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy by committing changes to the project repository. Material changes will be noted in the project changelog. Continued use of the service after changes constitutes acceptance.

10. Contact

For privacy inquiries, open an issue on the Scrolly GitHub repository or contact the project maintainers.
